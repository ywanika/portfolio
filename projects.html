Understanding Digital Design: Random Number Generator

January 2022, Honors Digital Electronics

In this project, I expanded upon the previous one so that a random number was generated. To do this, I added a clock, which would stop the number generator at a random point. This project gave me more depth into how clocks work, and consequently, a better understanding of some of the mechanisms behind different integrated circuits. In this project, designing the circuit on Multisim first was especially beneficial. It allowed me to understand how the circuit should be working and, by adding many probes, I could follow the path of the current. When placing the component on the breadboard, Mutlisim showed me which pins to use and I made sure to test the circuit at intervals.

Introduction to Sequential Logic Design

November 2021, Honors Digital Electronics

For this project, I designed a circuit which would iterate through the numbers 0-15 in binary. This project was my first time using a flip flop. This project was also my first time using an integrated circuit, so I learned a lot overall. I struggled with getting my circuit to work initially, redoing it a total of 3 times. Eventually, I figured out the breadboard wasn’t working in certain places by moving around the integrated circuit. I learned how to troubleshoot with a breadboard by creating simple circuits with LEDs as indicators to understand how current is moving within the breadboard. Before putting the pieces on the board, it was important to digitally design the circuit. I learned more features of Multisim, the circuit design software I used.

Swift Spritz

May 2021, AP Computer Science Applications

Annually, the Engineering Academy holds an Entrepreneurship Competition. Students will come up with a product to solve a problem, and they must prove to their peers and industry judges that their product is unique, marketable, and profitable. This year, my team designed a machine that automates the cleaning, disinfecting, sterilization, and pouching process for medical tools after we noticed the problem of high infection rates related to improper cleaning. The whole team worked heavily on design. We struggled on creating a compact and simple design that would also be better than the current solutions. I learned a lot about the process of brainstorming and designing, as we spent 9 out of the 16 days just sketching and went through 3 major iterations. Because I spent so much time getting to know the current systems and the needs of medical practices by talking to medical professionals, I guided the sketching process by suggesting features and we would work together to figure out how to implement them. We had started off with a 5-layer system, and then gradually brought it down to 1 layer with a pouching being opened at the bottom for the tools to fall into.

Aerodynamic Design

January 2020, Honors Principles Of Engineering

The purpose of this project was to recommend a projectile design. Our client, Nitro Planes, was looking to produce toy projectiles and wanted far-flying design recommendations. Each person in my team made two designs, and we would choose one design for our whole team to recommend. My main role in this project was to create my two paper rockets and write a design evaluation for each. I also delegated tasks and ensured the whole team stayed on task. I named my first design Jimmy Turner. In 8th-grade history class, we had learned that groves that went around the inside of the barrel of a gun, like a candy cane, helped bullets fly straighter and farther. I used this knowledge when designing Jimmy Turner; Jimmy Turner’s fins were slanted slightly to ensure it spun in the air, like a bullet. I learned from this experience and from seeing other people’s designs. For my second projectile, which I named Bar Code, I used duct tape on the outside of the rocket to secure the cone and I swept the wings back. In this project, I went through the design process and I improved my documentation skills.

Market Artists

March 2019, Computer Science Principles

The purpose of this project was to create a software in which a client would be able to manipulate images using a series of effects whose values could be changed. My partner and I chose our client to be a Phone Case Store that wanted a recognizable manipulation that could be applied to all their images. The client would add images to a folder, change the parameters to suit their likings, and run our code which asked them what manipulations they wanted to be applied on their images. The program would then save the manipulated images to a new folder. My partner and I evenly split the programming and documentation. We worked together closely and helped each other through bugs. My efforts were mainly focused on programming. I programmed the “triangle side”, “horizontal line”, and “drop shadow” effects, and I helped my partner with the “logo” and “Triangles & Rectangles” effects. I also worked on a feature that would allow multiple manipulations to be made to a singular image. This feature was harder to program. I tried many different approaches before I was finally able to find one that works. This project helped me improve my problem-solving skills and documentation skills.
